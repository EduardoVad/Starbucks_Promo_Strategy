# Starbucks Promotional Campaign — Who Should We Target?

## The Business Problem
Starbucks tested a $10 product promotion across 84,000+ customers. 
Sending it to everyone cost more than it returned — a net loss of $2,334. 
The business question: can we identify the right customers to make the promotion profitable?

## The Approach
Rather than promoting everyone, we analyzed the experiment data to find 
which customers actually changed their behavior because of the promotion.

We examined customer characteristics one by one to measure who responded 
most strongly to the promotion, then built a simple targeting rule based 
on the clearest patterns in the data.

## The Strategy
Target customers who match a specific combination of characteristics 
that showed the strongest incremental response to the promotion.

## The Result
| | Mass Promotion | Targeted Promotion |
|---|---|---|
| Customers reached | 41,650 (everyone) | 8,016 (19%) |
| Campaign result | -$2,334 loss | +$298 gain |
| Response lift | Baseline | 2.4x more effective |

By reaching fewer — but better — customers, the campaign flipped 
from unprofitable to profitable. A $2,632 swing in business outcome.
